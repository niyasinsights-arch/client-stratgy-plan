Verify before you publish

  • Eligibility, DGCA & licence terms — confirm current rules (age, Plus Two with Physics & Maths, medical class, exam subjects) and the CPL / ATPL / type-rating definitions on the official DGCA site before stating them.
  • Salary & pilot-demand figures — treat as approximate and cite a primary source (airlines, DGCA, ICAO/IATA, Boeing/Airbus outlooks) before putting numbers on screen.
  • Podcast facts — squawk 7500 & post-9/11 basics are verified; present MH370 theories as theories; recount Flight 1549 / Capt. Sullenberger factually with no invented quotes; verify every figure for Tenerife / AF447 / JAL123 and keep the tone respectful. Don’t reuse copyrighted ATC audio without rights.
  • South Africa claims — confirm current flying-hour, cost and visa specifics; they shift with regulations and exchange rates.
  • Real footage & consent (classroom visuals · M2 Day 3, airport visit · M2 Day 25) — use genuine photos/video of your own students and the captain, and get their written consent before posting. Don’t use stock or another academy’s footage as if it were yours.
  • Aviation-curiosity claims (e.g. why windows are round, why pilots say ‘Rotate’) — verify the historical detail (the de Havilland Comet / metal-fatigue story) before putting it on screen.

The goal is authority you can stand behind. Where a reliable source can’t be confirmed, soften the claim or leave it out rather than guess.
