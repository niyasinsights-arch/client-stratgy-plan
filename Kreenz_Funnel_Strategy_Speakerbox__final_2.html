Executive Overview

The core strategic truth: every competitor scores weakest on retention and community. So this architecture over-invests in the back half of the funnel — retention and referral — rather than only chasing reach. Shipping & COD Policy India Reality

Default: free shipping on all prepaid orders; ₹49 fee on COD. This nudges prepaid (better cash flow, far lower RTO risk) while still allowing COD for trust-cautious first-timers. Confirm COD address via WhatsApp before dispatch to cut RTO (return-to-origin) — the silent margin-killer for new Indian D2C brands. Keep the shipping rule identical everywhere; the audit flagged inconsistent free-shipping messaging.

Meta Ads Strategy
₹12,000/month (~₹400/day) — a signal-building budget, deployed by concentration
Blunt reality first. At ₹400/day you cannot run cold prospecting across 3 SKUs and full retargeting and expect Meta to gather the ~50 conversions/week per ad set it wants on "Purchase." Spreading this budget = no campaign ever exits the learning phase. So the strategy is concentration: one hero SKU for cold, everything else carried by organic + retargeting, and early optimisation on a cheaper signal than Purchase.

Landing Page Strategy
Sleep SKU as the template — built to fix the specific site-audit weaknesses
Fixes the weaknesses surfaced in the site audit: dual-branding confusion, broken certifications block, thin social proof, no reorder mechanic, inconsistent shipping messaging.

Section-by-Section Wireframe

  • Sticky header — Kreenz logo (brand-forward), nav, cart, persistent "Free shipping on prepaid" strip. Lead with Kreenz; "by Hand & Host LLP" lives small in trust/footer.
  • Hero — pain→promise headline ("Sleep through the night — naturally supported."), product shot, ₹729 (strike ₹899), primary CTA "Add to Cart," secondary "Subscribe & Save 15%." One trust cue above the fold.
  • Problem → reframe — "Melatonin alone gets you to sleep. Staying asleep needs more." Sets up the system.
  • The system (differentiator) — the 8 actives grouped by job (induce / calm / relax / recover). The moat; keep it skimmable.
  • Full label / transparency block — the entire ingredient panel, openly shown. Honesty is the brand.
  • Certifications block — FSSAI, GMP, ISO/HACCP, Halal, gluten-free, GMO-free as clean icons. Fix the broken block; verify every icon is substantiable.
  • Social proof — reviews with names + context ("Priya, 34, Bengaluru"), rating, count. Seed honestly; do not fabricate.
  • How to use / habit — "1 gummy, 30 min before bed." Reinforces the easy daily ritual.
  • Offer / subscribe — single-vs-subscribe toggle; subscription framed as default-smart.
  • FAQ — safety, non-habit-forming (factual, no over-claim), pregnancy/medication caution, shipping, returns, COD.
  • Risk reversal + mandatory disclaimer — satisfaction/support promise + the FSSAI disclaimer verbatim.
  • Final CTA + trust footer — repeat offer, identical shipping wording, contact/WhatsApp, "Marketed & fulfilled by Hand & Host LLP."

⚙️ CRO Recommendations

  • One primary CTA colour, repeated; remove competing buttons
  • Mobile-first; test thumb-reach CTA placement
  • Page speed: compress + lazy-load images
  • Subscription as visual default (don't hide one-time)
  • Sticky add-to-cart bar on mobile scroll
  • Exit-intent capture with first-order incentive
  • Consistent shipping + price messaging sitewide

🧠 Conversion Psychology (used honestly)

  • Anchoring: ₹899 strike-through vs ₹729
  • Transparency-as-trust: the full label disarms skepticism
  • Authority: certifications, plain-language science
  • Loss aversion: "never run out" subscription framing
  • Social proof: specific, named, contextual reviews
