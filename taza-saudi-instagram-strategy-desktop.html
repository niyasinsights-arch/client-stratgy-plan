The strategic gap: 953 posts but only ~21K followers signals high output, low velocity — feed-style content with weak shareability and almost no Reels-led reach or AI differentiation. Virtually no Saudi QSR is using AI storytelling yet. This plan leans into (1) viral, sound-led Reels for reach, (2) AI cinematic + realistic-human storytelling to own a creative lane competitors can't copy fast, (3) review proof + an interactive puzzle for trust & engagement, and (4) sharp value offers & combos to lift footfall, orders and the average bill.

Responding to Your Questions — Strategy Notes

AThe ASMR Reframe — Unboxing, Not CrunchReel #02 · #14AI
Why the change
You're right — TAZA broast is tender, not crunchy, so a "crunch ASMR" would misrepresent the product. The stronger, on-brand play is an unboxing ASMR that layers the sounds you do own: the chips crunch, the garlic-sauce squeeze & drizzle, the wrapper crinkle, the drink pour, the first tear of the pack.
How it serves the average-bill goal
Because it unboxes a full meal (broast + chips + sauce + drink), the "default order" people picture becomes bigger than a single item — nudging basket size up. Chips stay a supporting sound, not the hero.
Execution — hook · ear · eye · interactive · follow
  • Hook (0–1s): the pack lands on the table, "sound on 🔊" — immediate audio + close-up.
  • Ear: clean, layered ASMR captured with a close mic; no music fighting the sound.
  • Eye: macro reveal, steam, sauce drizzle in slow-mo — AI-assisted for a premium, seamless look.
  • Interactive: caption asks "which sound is the most satisfying — the pour, the squeeze, or the tear?" → comments.
  • Follow: end card "follow for the next unboxing" to build a repeatable series.
BVideo Length & RetentionRecommendation
Our recommendation
Yes — for most Reels, shorter is better. Aim for roughly 7–15 seconds, with the hook in the first 1–2 seconds and a clean loop so the video auto-replays. Reserve longer runtimes (20–45s) only for story-led pieces (the AI human story #08) and the hero brand film, where the emotion needs room to build.
Honest caveat
This is a general short-form best-practice and our professional judgment, not a guaranteed rule — shorter, fully-watched, looping videos tend to earn stronger completion signals, which usually helps reach. I'd recommend we validate it against TAZA's own numbers: watch the average-watch-time / retention graph in Instagram Insights for the first 2–3 weeks, then lock the ideal length from your real audience data rather than assumptions.
CThe Interactive Puzzle — Answer · Follow · Win 🏆Reel #06 · #18Motion GFX
Concept
A quick motion-graphics puzzle — e.g. "spot the hidden broast," a "find the odd sauce," or a fast "guess the item behind the blur." To enter, viewers answer in the comments, follow the page, and (optional) tag a friend. One winner gets a free meal / voucher 🏆.
Why it works
It stacks three growth signals — comments (engagement), follows (growth), and shares/tags (reach) — into one post, and the giveaway gives a real reason to act. Keep it to one clear rule set and announce the winner in Stories to close the loop.
Note
Please confirm the prize & any giveaway T&Cs from your side; we'll build the motion graphics.
DQSR Growth Playbook — Raising the Average BillYour ask
The core idea
In QSR, the average bill rarely moves because someone buys "more" — it moves because the default order gets bigger. So the content job is to make the full meal the mental default and to attach the natural add-ons every single time.
Tactics we'd run for TAZA
  • Always show the full meal: never post broast alone — broast + chips + garlic sauce + drink in-frame, so "a TAZA order" means a complete meal.
  • The add-on ritual: position garlic sauce & chips as "you can't have broast without…" — drives attach rate.
  • Price anchoring in combos: present a mid and a premium combo so the middle option reads as the value pick (client-supplied combo #10 is the vehicle).
  • Family / sharing formats: buckets & family boxes = bigger baskets, and they tie straight into the #05 "لمّة" culture post.
  • "Make it a meal" nudges: caption & Story prompts that upgrade a sandwich into a combo.
  • Day-part cravings: lunch-rush convenience & late-night hunger posts timed to peak order windows.
  • Delivery basket framing: "free delivery over X" style nudges (once you confirm thresholds) to lift online-order value.
  • Limited-time offers: seasonal LTOs to pull people up to premium items and create urgency.

Featured Hero Brand Film — Campaign Centerpiece

★ Hero Film "A Taste That Brings People Together" Brand Film / ReelLive-action (AI optional)Awareness
"A Taste That Brings People Together."
طعمٌ يجمعنا 🤍
Hook (Visual)
A man sits alone at the shore, the weight of life on his face — silence, sea, stillness.
Pillar · Objective
Saudi Lifestyle & Culture + Brand Storytelling · Brand awareness, emotional brand love & shareability
Content Concept
An emotional, near-wordless brand film about kindness and human connection. A man sits alone on the seashore facing the vast ocean, carrying the struggles and challenges of life. In the silence, a TAZA Broast Pack is gently placed beside him. He turns — an Arab man with a warm, gentle smile is standing there. No words are exchanged, but the gesture says everything: kindness, sharing, human connection. The man smiles back, opens the pack, and shares the meal. The film closes on both men sharing a moment of happiness overlooking the sea. This is TAZA's brand soul made visible — food as the thread that brings people together.
Detailed Execution Plan
  • Format & cuts: Produce a 45–60s hero film for the feed/Reels + a tight 20–30s social cut + a 6–10s teaser. Optional 15s vertical paid-ad version.
  • Act 1 (0–15s): Establish loneliness — wide shots of the lone man against the sea, slow push-in on his face, ambient waves only.
  • Act 2 (15–35s): The gesture — the TAZA pack placed beside him, his curious turn, the stranger's warm smile. Hold the silence; let the eyes carry it.
  • Act 3 (35–55s): Connection — they share the meal, the first laugh, two men side by side facing the ocean. Soft swell of music.
  • Close: Logo + tagline reveal "A Taste That Brings People Together / طعمٌ يجمعنا".
  • Casting & setting: Authentic Saudi coastal location (e.g., Yanbu/Red Sea — ties to TAZA's roots); relatable, real faces over models. Wardrobe natural/local.
  • Production options: (1) Live-action shoot for maximum authenticity, or (2) AI-assisted cinematic version (AI-generated coastal scenes + character consistency) for a faster, lower-cost cut — keep the human warmth intact either way.
  • Rollout: Launch as the pinned cornerstone, then tease via Stories; let the 12-post plan (esp. #5 culture & the client-side UGC) echo the same "bringing people together" theme for a unified month.
Visual Direction
Cinematic, restrained, emotionally warm. Golden-hour light over the sea; cool, muted tones in Act 1 warming as connection builds. Minimal text, natural sound design, a single tender music score. Real and human — no hard product push until the close. Shoot 9:16 for Reels + a 16:9 master for YouTube/paid.

Execution Notes & Growth Tactics

Posting & Growth

Lead with Reels for reach; post at 8–10pm KSA; reply to every comment in the first hour; use 3–5 niche + branded hashtags (#يعني_طازة anchored). Pin the cinematic (#01) & the hero film to the grid; keep most Reels short & loopable (see Video Length below).

Language

Arabic-first captions with light English support (matches the audience & existing @tazasaudi voice). Keep humour family-safe and culturally respectful for the Saudi market.

AI Production

Photorealistic humans only — no cartoon characters (per client direction; cartoon views trending down). Lock a consistent AI character reference across pieces, and keep all AI food shots true to the real product so reality matches the ad — protects trust.

Measurement

Awareness → reach, shares, follows. Consideration → saves, profile visits, link taps. Conversion → link clicks, code redemptions, "mention this post" orders. Review weekly; double down on the top 2 Reels with paid.
