The strategic arc — why this order

The plan is sequenced as a relationship, not a broadcast. You earn attention before you ask for the order. Each month graduates a stranger one step closer to becoming a repeat subscriber.

Month 1 — Awareness & Early Education

Name the pain (3 PM crashes, the Swiggy fast-food loop, low desk energy) and start teaching. You're not hard-selling yet — you're making them feel seen. Funnel: Awareness.

Month 2 — Education & Brand Trust

Become the smartest healthy-food account they follow, then earn belief — macro transparency, founder, kitchen, sourcing, real customers. Funnel: Consideration.

Month 3 — Offers, Reviews & Conversion

Now you ask. Subscription push, office-lunch targeting, comparisons, guilt-free dessert, community recap. Funnel: Conversion.

Experiential Campaign

The Zaladaa Healthy Challenge

A public fitness game taken to where the crowds already are — parks, beaches, walking tracks, colleges, IT parks and malls. Anyone can play, win a reward, and — most importantly — film it. It turns ordinary foot traffic into shop visitors, your neighbourhood into content creators, and "The Fresh Habit" into something people physically do, not just scroll past.

More shop visitors More UGC reels Stronger brand recall Low cost · high shareability
The Concept

Move a little, win a bowl

Run a public health-challenge activity in high-footfall locations where people are already out and active. They join a simple game, complete a quick task, and instantly win a reward. The genius is inclusivity: "100 pull-ups" excludes 95% of people, but a 30-second plank or 10 squats invites everyone — which means more participants, more laughter, and far more footage worth posting.

Where to set up

Target high-footfall public spaces: parks, beaches, walking tracks, colleges, IT parks, malls, and busy public stretches. Match the spot to the audience — IT parks & malls at lunch for working professionals, walking tracks & parks in the morning/evening for the fitness crowd, colleges for young UGC creators. Carry a banner + QR so onlookers who don't play can still find you. (Quick check: confirm any permission needed for the specific public spot before setting up.)

Pick-your-challenge menu

Let people choose their level so no one feels left out — or let a spin-the-wheel assign the task for extra fun.

  • Easy — 10 squats
  • Easy — 20 jumping jacks
  • Medium — 30-second plank
  • Anyone — a mini "healthy habit" challenge (name 3 fresh ingredients, swap a soda for a juice)
  • Finale — spin-the-wheel for the reward after completing the task

Reward ideas

Everyone wins something — the variable reward is what makes it addictive and re-postable. Load the wheel with:

  • 10% off coupon
  • Free add-on / topping
  • Buy 1 Get 1 on select items
  • Free healthy drink with lunch
  • Special combo-meal offer
  • Voucher for the next visit (drives the return trip)
  • Instagram feature + surprise gift for the best participant
Campaign name

The Zaladaa Healthy Challenge

Run of Show

How to actually run it

Keep it tight so the line keeps moving and the energy stays high. One host with a phone can run the whole thing.

1 · The hook

A standee / chalkboard at your public spot: "Do 10 squats. Win lunch. 👀" — curiosity stops passers-by mid-walk. Keep a QR banner visible so onlookers can find you too.

2 · The challenge

Person picks or spins for a task (squats / jumping jacks / plank / habit question). 30–45 seconds, max.

3 · The film

Host films it vertically (with their OK) — fun, energetic, reaction-led. This footage is the real product of the campaign.

4 · The reward

Spin the wheel → instant win. Hand over the coupon/voucher with a genuine "come see us for lunch."

5 · The loop

Ask them to tag @zaladaa and use a campaign hashtag when they post. Reshare every tag to your story — that public reshare is what pulls the next person in.

Why It Works

The strategic payoff

More shop visitors

A free, fun reason to engage — and a voucher that pulls the visit to the shop.

More UGC content

Every participant becomes a content creator. You harvest a week of reels in one session.

Reels & stories

Movement + reward + reaction is inherently watchable — exactly the format the algorithm rewards.

Page engagement

Tags, reshares and the hashtag loop spike comments, shares and profile visits.

Brand recall

People remember what they did, not what they read. The Fresh Habit becomes a physical memory.

Community proof

Crowds seeing others play signals "this is a brand people love" — the trust shortcut.

How it plugs into your 3-month plan

This isn't a standalone stunt — it fuels the feed. Tease it in early stories ("something fun is coming"), run the activation mid-plan, then the footage feeds your Post 19 (real customer) and Post 20 (community) trust posts, plus a string of UGC reels and a story highlight under "Events." Add a geo-targeted Meta boost to the best participant reel for cheap, high-relevance reach.

Strategy Goal

What this campaign is built to do

Increase shop visits, trial orders, and repeat customers by making Zaladaa feel like a fun, healthy, and shareable food brand. The campaign creates curiosity in public spaces, brings people into the store, and turns the brand into a healthy weekend food habit.

Core Campaign — Protein Menu Drop

This campaign works like a surprise invitation. You place or drop coupons in public or high-footfall areas around the city, and each coupon gives access to a special protein menu at Zaladaa.

The coupon should feel like a hidden pass rather than a regular discount. This creates curiosity and encourages people to visit the shop and try the menu.

Big Idea

Swap Your Sunday for a Healthy Sunday.

This positions Zaladaa as the place where people replace heavy weekend meals with a fresh, protein-rich option. It works especially well because weekend food choices are more flexible, and people are often open to trying something new.

Campaign Structure

  • Drop coupons in public or high-footfall locations
  • Make the coupon look like an exclusive healthy invitation
  • Offer a protein menu / exclusive menu available only to coupon holders
  • Clearly display the protein value on the menu — such as 20g, 25g, or 30g of protein
  • Provide a small reward or free add-on for customers who redeem the coupon

Offer Design

Simple and attractive:

  • Special protein bowl
  • Healthy Sunday menu
  • Free add-on with coupon
  • Limited-time weekend offer
  • Next-visit coupon for customers who redeem the offer

The menu should feel premium yet easy to understand — customers should instantly recognise it's healthy, protein-packed, and worth trying.

Content Plan

The campaign should generate content at every stage:

  • Show the coupon drop in Reels or Stories
  • Record people finding or receiving the coupon
  • Capture customer reactions when they visit the shop
  • Film the food being prepared and served
  • Repost customer Stories and UGC to build social proof

This lets the campaign work twice — once for increasing footfall, again for expanding social-media reach.

Visual Direction

Fresh, clean, and healthy:

  • Use green, white, and natural food colours
  • Showcase protein bowls and fresh ingredients clearly
  • Keep the coupon design simple yet premium
  • Use bold typography for protein quantities
  • Include Sunday / weekend visual cues to feel timely and relevant
